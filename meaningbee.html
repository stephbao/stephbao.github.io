Meaning Bee

Design Research Assistant (Independent Study)

Spring 2018

Context

Meaning-Bee, tests a player's word senses—the different senses of a word based on its dictionary definition. For example, the word "bank" has many senses: a financial institution, the bank of a river, a bank shot in basketball, etc.

I worked on this project with Anthony Tomasic and John Zimmerman at Carnegie Mellon University.

Goals (For the Game)

  • Be intellectually stimulating for participants
  • Be entertaining for an audience
  • Generate interesting computational and linguistic information

Process

Throughout the process, we worked with two classes in the Steel Valley School District in the Pittsburgh region. We visited each class three times.

First Visit

We surveyed the students to better understand the context in which they play games, what types of games they like, and how they study.

Second Visit

We held co-design sessions. We split the classes into 3 teams. Each team was given a list of words and a set of tools to create a word game. After 15 minutes, each team presented their game to then rest of the class. We focused on understanding the mechanics of each game and what they found to be exciting.


Third Visit

We made more formal versions of two games created by the students in the second visit and gave them to the students to play. We took notes of the features the students like, what they wished could change, and the interactions between the students.
