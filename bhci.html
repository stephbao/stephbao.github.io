Visualizing Patient Generated Health Data

BHCI Capstone Project

Spring 2018

Context

The HCI Undergraduate Project course is a semester-long capstone project for the HCI program, in which students work in interdisciplinary teams for a client to produce a working prototype that serves as a proof of concept of a novel service or product idea.

Client

Our client was Dr. Carissa Low, the Principle Investigator at the Biobehavioral Oncology and Technology Lab at the University of Pittsburgh. Her team has been collecting patient generated health data through AWARE framework and Fitbit, and hopes to be able to visualize these data for clinicians to be reviewed in post operative visits.

Role

I was the UX Lead for this project, in which I created low, medium, and high fidelity prototypes using pen and paper, Sketch, and InVision and synthesized findings from our user testing sessions.

Process

We spent a semester conducting interviews, contextual inquiries, surveys, and think-alouds with primarily nurses and physician assistants to understand their needs.
